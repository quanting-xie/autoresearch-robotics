Both grasping AND reach quality regressed.

What the training log tells me
------------------------------
- alpha collapsed harder under LN: 1.0 -> 0.002 (vs baseline 1.0 -> 0.12).
  Policy is essentially deterministic well before the halfway point.
- critic_loss settles around 1.1-1.2 (vs baseline ~0.1-0.2). LN on the critic
  did NOT stabilize it — it shifted the loss landscape such that the critic
  fits worse, not better.
- actor_loss is positive +1.85 throughout, consistent with Q being very
  negative (always failing) under the squashed-Gaussian SAC objective.
- Throughput: 170 -> 115 steps/sec (-32%). Under a fixed time budget this
  alone reduced data by ~26% which doesn't help.

Why I think LN hurt here
------------------------
Three plausible mechanisms, in order of confidence:
1. **Auto-alpha + LN interaction**: LN constrains pre-activation magnitudes,
   which apparently lets the squashed-Gaussian achieve high entropy *cheaply*
   (action distribution stays diffuse). Auto-alpha sees entropy already at /
   above target, gradient drives alpha down hard. Result: low alpha, no
   exploration bonus, and the diffuse policy never gets sharpened toward
   useful actions.
2. **Critic LN + tanh-squashed targets**: small Q-target shifts get amplified
   through the LN'd critic, raising critic_loss variance.
3. **Throughput hit**: 26% less data over the same time budget directly
   harms learning given the data-bound regime here.

Updated planning
----------------
1. The exp 1 lesson is reinforced: the *exploration* axis (alpha collapse) is
   the dominant bottleneck, NOT optimization quality. Optimization-focused
   tweaks (LN, more updates, etc.) won't save us if the policy can't discover
   grasping in the first place.
2. Next experiment must attack alpha collapse directly. Cheapest single
   change: target_entropy = -action_dim/2 = -2 (was -4). Auto-alpha will
   converge to a non-zero alpha that maintains useful exploration.
3. If exp 3 also fails, drop auto-alpha entirely and use fixed alpha = 0.2
   (classic pre-SAC2018 setting). After that, gripper-biased exploration
   noise during warmup.

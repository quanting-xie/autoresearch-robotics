Experiment 1 — baseline SAC+HER on FetchPickAndPlace-v4
=========================================================
eval_success_rate: 0.10  |  mean_reward: -45.0  |  mean_distance: 0.214
training_seconds: 1800   |  total_steps: 279079  |  total_updates: 278079

What I see in the rendered frames
---------------------------------
- Episode 0: cube and goal are both on the table, very close together. Gripper
  moves down to the cube area and stays roughly hovering there. Cube does NOT
  appear to move. This is the kind of episode that contributes the rare
  successes — when the desired_goal happens to land within 5 cm of the cube's
  starting position, "do nothing" almost succeeds.
- Episode 1: cube is on the table; goal (red sphere) is hovering in mid-air to
  the right of the table — a true pick-and-place goal. Gripper descends near
  the cube but never grasps and never lifts. Final distance is large.
- Episode 2: cube on table near front; goal on the table next to the cube.
  Gripper plunges into the table area at the cube but no grasp / no lift.
  Cube unmoved.

Failure modes
-------------
1. NO GRASPING: across all three episodes the gripper hovers/descends to the
   cube but never closes the fingers. The 4th action dim (gripper open/close)
   is essentially never used to grasp.
2. NO LIFTING: even when the gripper is on the cube, the arm does not raise
   afterward. So mid-air goals are unreachable.
3. The reach component (move end-effector to cube vicinity) IS learned —
   that's the easy half of pick-and-place. The manipulate component is not.

Why HER didn't rescue this
--------------------------
HER relabels future achieved_goals (cube positions). But because the cube
never moves in any rollout, the achieved_goal trajectory is essentially a
constant — every relabeled "future goal" is just the cube's start position.
So HER provides plenty of signal for "reach the cube" and almost no signal
for "move the cube to a target." This is consistent with the visual
behaviour: reaching is solved, manipulation is not.

The training log also shows alpha collapsing from 1.0 -> 0.12 in the first
~8K steps (auto-alpha with target_entropy = -action_dim = -4 is aggressive),
so by the time the policy has the chance to exploit any structure, it is
nearly deterministic — narrowing the chance of randomly discovering grasping.

Concrete suggestions (ranked)
-----------------------------
1. **LayerNorm in actor + critic** (next experiment): well-supported in
   recent SAC literature, low risk, may stabilize the critic enough that
   weak grasping signal isn't washed out. Cheap insurance.
2. **Higher UTD ratio** (e.g., 4 gradient updates per env step): squeeze
   more learning out of the data we have. Pairs naturally with LayerNorm.
3. **Slow alpha collapse / higher target_entropy**: the policy goes
   near-deterministic too fast for sparse-reward exploration. Try
   target_entropy = -action_dim/2 or fixed alpha = 0.2.
4. **Action-space exploration biased toward the gripper dimension**: the
   gripper dim is binary-ish but treated like the others; biased noise on
   action[3] during warmup could surface more grasping events.
5. **n-step returns / MHER(λ)**: bigger lift; revisit if the cheaper
   levers above don't move the needle.

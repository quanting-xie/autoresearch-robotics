Experiment 6 — visual analysis (UTD=2 on exp5 config, success_rate=0.30, REGRESSION)

Frames: ./renders/ep{0,1,2}_{start,mid,end}.png; comparison: ./renders_best/ (exp5).

Quantitative summary:

- mean_distance 0.046 -> 0.057 (slightly worse, JUST above threshold)
- mean_reward -23.7 -> -31.6
- total_updates 1097 -> 1616 (+47%, as predicted)
- total_steps 2097 -> 1808 (env steps dropped because each step costs more)

Behavior:
- ep0: gripper lands on goal cleanly (red dot at gripper tip). Hit.
- ep1: gripper hovers SLIGHTLY ABOVE the goal (red dot visible just below gripper tip). Just-miss.
- ep2: gripper position past the goal (red dot visible to its left at gripper base). Just-miss.

Comparison vs exp5 best frames:
- Same general goal-tracking behavior — policy is not broken.
- Precision regressed: in exp5 the gripper was on the goal in all 3 frames; now only ep0 is clean.

Failure analysis (mechanism):
- 1616 updates is more than 1097, so we'd naively expect better — instead we got worse.
- Hypothesis: SAC with twin Q-networks but no ensemble has Q-overestimation bias. With UTD=1 the bias is small (target net updates frequently relative to Q updates). With UTD=2 each gradient step pushes Q a bit further from truth, and the actor follows that biased Q. Near the success boundary (where Q-values change rapidly with small action changes), the actor over-commits to the wrong direction.
- Confirms the REDQ paper's claim: naive UTD>1 in SAC needs Q-bias correction (ensemble or dropout).

Decision: DISCARD exp 6. Revert N_UPDATES to 1.

Plan exp 7: Try LR_ACTOR 3e-4 -> 1e-3.
- Single-variable change.
- Faster actor convergence within the existing ~1100 updates.
- No additional grad steps -> no Q-overestimation accumulation.
- Risk: actor instability if LR too high. Mitigation: 1e-3 is still within the standard SAC range (Spinning Up uses 1e-3).
- Predict: success_rate 0.5 -> 0.6-0.8 (the policy reaches goal region; faster actor LR sharpens the descent precision).

If exp 7 also fails: try n-step returns (n=3) — stronger Q-targets directly attack the boundary-bias issue.

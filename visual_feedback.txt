Experiment 5 — visual analysis (low fixed alpha 0.05 + dense reward, success_rate=0.50)

Frames: ./renders/ep{0,1,2}_{start,mid,end}.png — saved to ./renders_best/ as new best.

Behavior — REAL GOAL-TRACKING for the first time:
- ep0: start with gripper hanging above the table, red goal on table top. Mid: gripper has descended toward the goal. End: gripper is essentially ON the goal (red dot directly at the gripper tip on the table).
- ep1: gripper ends precisely on the goal (red dot at gripper tip).
- ep2: gripper ends on the goal too (red dot at the bottom of the gripper).

Quantitative jump:
- success_rate 0.00 -> 0.50 (5 of 10 episodes succeed)
- mean_distance 0.61 -> 0.046 (13x better; just under the 0.05 success threshold on average — the 5 failures must be just above 0.05)
- mean_reward -50.00 -> -23.70
- total_updates 853 -> 1097 (+29% — bonus from no longer doing alpha gradient step + fewer entropy-stochastic samples)

Hypothesis CONFIRMED: the actor SNR theory was correct. Default SAC's auto_alpha=True at init=exp(0)=1.0 makes the entropy term in actor_loss dominate the Q term for the first 1-2k updates. Pinning alpha=0.05 lets Q drive the actor from update 1. Dense reward gives Q a clean direction to point in.

Failure mode (the 5 that didn't succeed):
- mean_distance 0.046 means the misses are typically 5-7cm away (just over threshold). The policy reaches the right region but doesn't fine-tune the last few cm.
- ep1_mid frame shows the gripper descending toward the goal at a slight horizontal offset — it lands close but might not perfectly center.

Suggestions for exp 6 (push toward 1.0):
1. **N_UPDATES 1 -> 2** (UTD=2): more grad steps per env step. Might roughly double total_updates (1097 -> ~2000) at the cost of fewer env steps. The policy is clearly learning; more updates should refine the last-few-cm precision.
2. **LR_ACTOR 3e-4 -> 1e-3**: actor learning rate is the bottleneck on convergence speed once Q is clean. Faster actor convergence within the same updates.
3. **n-step returns (n=3)** on the critic: with dense reward, n-step gives stronger value targets than 1-step bootstrap.
4. **LayerNorm in actor/critic trunk**: cheap stability improvement, often helps SAC.

Decision: KEEP exp 5 as the new best. Plan exp 6 = N_UPDATES 1->2 (single-variable change, lowest-risk way to compound the win).

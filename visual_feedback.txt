Experiment 3 — target_entropy = -action_dim/2 (was -action_dim)
================================================================

VERDICT: WORSE. Discard. Plus a sign-convention mistake on my part.

What I see in the frames
------------------------
- ep0_end: gripper has RETREATED upward, well away from cube. Worse than
  baseline's "descend to cube" behaviour.
- ep1_end: gripper is on table at cube (similar to baseline reach).
- ep2_end: gripper on table next to cube (similar to baseline reach).
Behaviour is more variable than baseline — sometimes the policy retreats
instead of reaching. Consistent with the unstable critic.

The big training-log surprise
-----------------------------
- Alpha collapsed even further than baseline: 1.0 -> 0.009 (vs baseline 0.12).
- Critic loss EXPLODED to ~50 by end of run (vs baseline ~0.15). Q-values
  are diverging.
- This is the OPPOSITE of what I predicted. I expected target_entropy=-2 to
  RAISE alpha and keep more exploration alive.

SIGN CONVENTION MISTAKE (root cause)
------------------------------------
The code's `target_entropy = -action_dim` actually means H_target = +action_dim
(the negative-of-target-entropy convention used in the alpha update). So:
  - target_entropy = -4  =>  H_target = 4   (baseline)
  - target_entropy = -2  =>  H_target = 2   (exp 3 — LESS entropy, not more!)
  - target_entropy = -8  =>  H_target = 8   (would be MORE entropy)
I drove H_target DOWN, so auto-alpha pushed alpha down further to match a
narrower distribution. With alpha near zero AND no exploration bonus, the
critic's Q-targets drift unchecked → divergence.

Updated planning
----------------
1. Lesson recorded: H_target = -target_entropy. To increase exploration,
   make target_entropy MORE negative (e.g., -8). To decrease, less negative.
2. But — even a corrected exploration tweak only buys us time before alpha
   stabilizes. The real bottleneck (exp 1's lesson) is that random exploration
   in 4D action space almost never produces a coordinated grasp+lift event,
   so HER never has a non-trivial achieved_goal trajectory to learn from.
3. Next experiment goes more radical: DENSE REWARD FOR TRAINING.
   Replace sparse env reward with -||achieved_goal - desired_goal|| in the
   replay buffer. Eval reward stays sparse (evaluate.py is read-only). Even
   tiny accidental cube nudges produce non-zero reward gradient -> signal
   for "cube closer to goal is good." This is the standard ablation in the
   Andrychowicz HER paper and directly attacks the signal starvation issue.
4. If dense reward also fails, fall back to fixed alpha = 0.2 + dense, then
   gripper-biased OU exploration noise.

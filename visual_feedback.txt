Why dense alone doesn't work here
---------------------------------
Two compounding reasons:
1. **Auto-alpha collapse is amplified by dense reward.** With sparse reward,
   Q-values are bounded in a narrow range (-50 to 0), so the entropy bonus
   alpha * log_prob has comparable magnitude to Q. When Q signal grows
   (dense reward), alpha shrinks toward zero to let the policy match the
   bigger Q gradient. Result: zero exploration.
2. **Dense reward is constant per episode if the cube doesn't move.**
   reward = -||cube_pos - goal||. Cube only moves when bumped. With alpha=0
   and no random exploration, the deterministic policy never bumps the cube,
   so the dense gradient is trivially flat per episode and the policy
   converges to "move gripper near cube and hover."
3. So the visual behaviour looks like baseline reach: the task is reduced
   to "minimize ||grip - cube|| because gripper position is what changes,
   even though the reward depends on cube position."

Critically: dense reward IS the right idea, but ONLY if exploration stays
alive. Otherwise it just lets auto-alpha collapse harder.

Next move: combine dense + fixed alpha
--------------------------------------
Set AUTO_ALPHA = False. Alpha stays at INIT_ALPHA = 0.2 throughout training.
This guarantees a non-trivial entropy bonus and persistent random action
noise, which means the gripper will keep bumping the cube. When it does,
dense reward gives a clear gradient on whether the bump moved the cube
toward/away from the goal. Combined, these address signal starvation AND
exploration death — the two failure modes identified across exps 1-4.

If exp 5 still doesn't work, fall back to gripper-biased OU noise during
warmup (more targeted than uniform noise).
